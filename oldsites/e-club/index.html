Purpose
 The Engineers' Club is the official organization of OU's College of Engineering. E-Club was founded in 1910 and is today, one of the largest student organizations on campus, consisting of over 3000 members of which 500 are currently active.

The purpose of the Engineers' Club is to provide a social outlet while promoting a better fellowship among students, faculty, alumni, and professional engineers and to increase the future engineer's knowledge of engineering in all of its phases. This includes communication, organization, and participation, as well as training in technical matters. There are plenty of clubs and technical societies that are looking for good students who are willing to put some time and work into setting our school apart from the rest.

You may be asking yourself, “How do I become a member of E-Club?” The answer is simply to major in Engineering. You do not pay dues, we provide transportation if needed, and we have free food at all our General Meetings, so join us and relax from the stress of being an Engineering Student! We have many events throughout the year, ranging from our monthly General Meetings to our week-long celebration of Engineers Week. So come out and join us, and be ready to have some fun!
 
 
For more information please contact eclub@ou.edu
